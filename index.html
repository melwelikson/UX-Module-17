ABOUT



Mel Welikson is a visuals editor and photographer with 7+ years of experience in the field of photo editing, designing graphics, social media editing, and production. She is highly skilled with a background in Studio Art and a focus on photography. Currently pursuing a UI/UX Bootcamp Certification from Columbia Engineering, she has a strong foundation in visual design and a keen eye for aesthetics. During her tenure at The New Yorker as a Digital Photo Editor, Mel excelled in sourcing visually captivating photographs for stories and managing the photo department's Instagram account. Prior to The New Yorker, Mel held the role of Associate Editor at photograph magazine, and Production Work Scholar at Aperture Foundation.

With expertise in photography, design, production, and content creation, she thrives in a dynamic and creative environment, bringing a passion for visual storytelling and a proficiency in digital platforms.

Digital Skills: Adobe Photoshop, Bridge, InDesign, Lightroom, InCopy, Illustrator, Figma, WordPress, CoPilot, FTP, Procreate, Microsoft Office, AirTable, HTML, CSS, Final Cut Pro, SmartSheet
